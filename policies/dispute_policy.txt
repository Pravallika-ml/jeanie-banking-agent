Transaction Dispute Policy

Customers can dispute a transaction they don't recognize, believe is
fraudulent, or that reflects goods/services not received. Disputes must be
filed within 60 days of the transaction date to be eligible under
Regulation E protections.

Once a dispute is filed, the bank issues a provisional credit to the
customer's account within 10 business days while the investigation is
ongoing. The investigation itself takes up to 45 days for most transactions,
or up to 90 days for disputes involving a new account (open less than 30
days) or a transaction that occurred outside the United States.

If the dispute is resolved in the customer's favor, the provisional credit
becomes permanent. If resolved against the customer, the provisional credit
is reversed and the customer is notified in writing with an explanation.
